About Me

I am a dedicated designer and visual artist looking for opportunities to learn and put my skills to good use. I have a background in visual arts and graphics but I’m looking for opportunities as a multimedia designer, where I can use the skills that I already have but also the ones that I recently acquired from KEA University. I am currently ending an old chapter and starting a new one together with KEA. I am looking for opportunities for an internship. I am finishing Multimedia Design and Communication at KEA, and looking for a dedicated company/person such as myself, to work with.

What am I looking for? Interested in working with editing, graphic design, images, drawing, prototypes, ideation, UX, UI design. Also I would love to work with HTML and CSS together with these design tasks that I am sure I can achieve. Out of the comfort zone and also willing to give it a try is animation, maybe also web animation, gaming, character design, etc. I am open to learning anything related to multimedia.

Due to the current situation now in 2020, everything needs to be flexible and we need to be creative with how we work. I am looking for a job/internship where I can also work from home, with a devoted team or person. If working from home is not possible, I am willing to travel to work, either in Copenhagen region or West London region.

My creative process


Today, my interest is being drawn by technology and multimedia so I truly believe that all the practical experience I’ve had as a visual artist, with creating different kinds of artwork, managing projects and branching out to new things, has brought me to this point in time that urges me to take a different path into design.
So as the world keeps changing its means of communication, its quality and disadvantage of being dependent on the digitisation process urges us to step up and create new design solutions.

Here is a page I made from scratch, to show you my personal creative process. From idea to the final product there is a complex journey that requires both the designer and the user to communicate efficiently. There are many ways to do that, now the important question is, what is the message?
