About Myself

My Journey

My name is Nina Popovici, I am a dedicated fellow artist. Growing up I did taste art since early childhood when I started to imagine and draw the bedtime stories told by my parents and grandparents. At the beginning I have started small, on scrap paper, then in one day, exhausting all the pages, I was looking at the walls in my room, then with a smile and exploratory heart I dared to expand my visions on those 4 immaculate “canvases”.

I have created my own virtual world, timeless, with my heroes, unseen creatures and surreal, all imagined, deformed and transformed by my own understanding and imagination ... I have been in love with drawing ever since, as it offered me inner peace, smiles and serenity, things that the majority of us are craving. My background is mostly visual art, as I have been studying in schools with an art profile. The first environment was “Octav Bancila Highschool Of Visual Arts”, where I learned to sharpen my skills and perceive the qualities from the environment/subject.

After highschool was University, where I spent 3 years deepening the knowledge based in the highschool years followed by the discovery of new technologies and ways of working with visual media.

Teaching others on my journey

I started working more with mural painting and getting a lot of commissions and clients, as self employed. After a while, one of the companies that I was painting for was a private school/nursing home and they needed a teacher for their kindergartens visual art classes. The potential behind this was quickly uncovered so I was offered many more hours and classes to conduct with different year students.

Visual communication and expression is a skill and was very important to teach by doing. That way, my students remembered the times they tried something new, failed and got frustrated and then, achieved amazing results by trying themselves. After these two years I had to relocate to Denmark and I left my teaching and painting job in Romania.

Learning a new language is another whole process in itself. Denmark offered me a new set of challenges as well as a new set of skills. Unlike other roles I had before, this time I had to branch out into the unknown and try my luck. I spent a lot of time studying the market for painting material prices, opportunities for exhibitions and projects.

My relation with technology

I started using a computer at an early age and was fascinated when the internet appeared, as at the time I was playing computer games like Red alert, Warcraft, Starcraft, but most of all by the visual graphics, which at the time were quite primitive. As the internet rolled in, we started communicating with people from other countries relatively easily, we managed to stream music online and see the very first internet web pages, basically learning a new way to communicate and think.

I started using technologies like Adobe, became curious of colour schemes, gradients and the million ways you could play with an image. After highschool I kept my graphics profile and went on to study at “George Enescu University” where I began to go in-depth in working with many kinds of media. From hand-drawing to digital drawing and photography, graphic design and printing. Next up on my list was Illustrator. From drawing and concept to the actual design, where I could easily see many possible options with the click of a button. This took me to the next level, creatively.

After a few years of working with Adobe and trying out Corel I got a job at a Printing company in London. Here, I applied the knowledge I had in university and I learned much more about printing, since my role was digital print operator. My job was to create simple designs for flyers, business cards, etc. Printing sounds easy but in order to create a good quality product, you must work very hard. This includes many attempts, from which I learned when I was in the 3 month training period. As curiosity took over me, I applied for the Multimedia program in Copenhagen where I would deepen my relationship with technology and learn how I can use my background to create innovative design solutions!

What can I offer?

To improve the quality of design and bring innovative ideas to life. Also, finding solutions for the problems we are currently facing is our first priority and duty.

My goal is creating new, efficient and pleasing aesthetic design solutions but also applications that can help our society and private lives thrive and overcome any hardship. My values are mutual respect and open communication, with the expectations laid out on the table. Respect for our planet and animals, by recycling and having environmentally friendly ideas to work with. Punctuality and consistency help me reach my greatest potential in work. I am a very flexible person if needed, but I prefer to have a fixed stable routine.

My favourite digital tools are Adobe Cloud, HTML, CSS, a bit of JS. I would like to work with animation, character design, graphic design, maybe games. Editing, drawing, organising, writing and design is what I would prefer to do but I am open to learning new things.
